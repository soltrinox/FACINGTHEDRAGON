## **EMERGENCY GROUNDING TECHNIQUES**

### **5-4-3-2-1 Sensory Grounding**
1. Name 5 things you can see
2. Name 4 things you can touch
3. Name 3 things you can hear
4. Name 2 things you can smell
5. Name 1 thing you can taste

### **Box Breathing**
1. Breathe in for 4 counts
2. Hold for 4 counts
3. Breathe out for 4 counts
4. Hold for 4 counts
5. Repeat 4-6 times

### **Physical Grounding**
- Stand up and stretch
- Splash cold water on your face
- Hold something solid (pen, book, table edge)
- Press your feet into the floor
- Tense and release muscles

### **Mental Grounding**
- Count backwards from 100 by 7s
- Name all the states/provinces you can remember
- Recite something you know by heart
- Describe your surroundings in detail

## **COMMON TRIGGER RESPONSES AND ALTERNATIVES**

### **When You Feel Disrespected**

**Old Response:**
- Immediate anger
- Demanding respect
- Escalating conflict
- Threatening or intimidating

**New Response:**
- Pause and breathe
- Ask: "Is this actually disrespect or am I reacting to an old wound?"
- Communicate: "When you [behavior], I feel [emotion]. Can we talk about this?"
- Set boundary: "I need you to [specific behavior]. If you can't, I'll [consequence]."

### **When You Feel Abandoned**

**Old Response:**
- Panic and cling
- Test the person
- Push them away first
- Assume the worst

**New Response:**
- Pause and breathe
- Ask: "Is this person actually abandoning me or am I reacting to past abandonment?"
- Check reality: "What evidence do I have that they're leaving?"
- Communicate need: "I'm feeling anxious about our connection. Can we talk?"

### **When You Feel Criticized**

**Old Response:**
- Get defensive immediately
- Attack back
- Shut down completely
- Assume they're attacking your character

**New Response:**
- Pause and breathe
- Ask: "Is this criticism or feedback? Is it about my behavior or my worth?"
- Listen first: "Can you tell me more about what you mean?"
- Respond thoughtfully: "I hear you. Let me think about that."

### **When You Feel Angry**

**Old Response:**
- Explode immediately
- Blame others
- Use anger to control
- Suppress until it explodes

**New Response:**
- Pause and breathe
- Ask: "What is my anger protecting? What am I really feeling?"
- Name the underlying emotion: "I'm feeling hurt/scared/powerless"
- Express the need: "I need [specific need]. Can we address this?"

### **When You Feel Overwhelmed**

**Old Response:**
- Push through anyway
- Numb out
- Shut down completely
- Panic

**New Response:**
- Stop and use grounding
- Acknowledge: "I'm overwhelmed right now"
- Take a break: "I need to pause and come back to this"
- Use your safety plan

## **INTERNAL DIALOGUE QUICK-REFERENCE**

### **Abandonment Thoughts**

**Unhealthy:**
- "Everyone always leaves me"
- "I'm not worth staying for"
- "If they really cared, they would..."

**Healthy:**
- "Some people stay, some people leave, and that's about them, not my worth"
- "My worth doesn't depend on others' presence"
- "I can communicate my needs and accept others' limitations"

### **Self-Worth Thoughts**

**Unhealthy:**
- "I'm fundamentally broken"
- "I have to prove my worth"
- "I'm not as good as others"

**Healthy:**
- "I'm wounded, not broken, and wounds can heal"
- "My worth is inherent and doesn't require proof"
- "I'm on my own unique path, and that's valid"

### **Anger Thoughts**

**Unhealthy:**
- "Anger is the only safe emotion"
- "I shouldn't feel this way"
- "If I show emotion, I'll be rejected"

**Healthy:**
- "I can feel all my emotions, not just anger"
- "All my emotions are valid and informative"
- "I can express emotions safely with the right people"

### **Control Thoughts**

**Unhealthy:**
- "I have to control everything to stay safe"
- "I have to be perfect to be acceptable"
- "If I don't do it right, it's not worth doing"

**Healthy:**
- "I can influence things, but I don't need to control everything"
- "I'm human, and humans are imperfect, and that's okay"
- "Trying is more important than perfect results"

## **PATTERN RECOGNITION CHEAT SHEET**

### **Abandonment Pattern**
- Pushing people away before they can leave
- Testing people to see if they'll stay
- Expecting rejection
- Using abandonment as justification

**Key Question:** "Am I reacting to present reality or past abandonment?"

### **Anger Pattern**
- Anger covering fear, hurt, or shame
- Reacting to old wounds, not present situations
- Using anger to control or intimidate
- Suppressing other emotions

**Key Question:** "What is my anger protecting? What am I really feeling?"

### **Control Pattern**
- Trying to control things you can't control
- Perfectionism and fear of mistakes
- Controlling others' behavior
- Inability to tolerate uncertainty

**Key Question:** "What can I actually control in this situation?"

### **People-Pleasing Pattern**
- Giving from emptiness, not abundance
- Expecting reciprocation for giving
- Sacrificing your needs for others
- Exhaustion from overgiving

**Key Question:** "Am I giving freely or hoping to receive something in return?"

### **Self-Worth Pattern**
- Trying to prove your worth
- Basing value on others' approval
- Comparing yourself to others
- Believing you're fundamentally broken

**Key Question:** "Is my worth inherent or does it need to be earned?"

## **KEY CONCEPTS**

### **The Dragon**
Your accumulated unhealed wounds, protective patterns, and survival strategies. Not your enemy, but a guardian that needs understanding.

### **The Three You's**
- The Child You (emotional, reactive, forming beliefs)
- The Teen/Young Adult You (proving, masking, seeking belonging)
- The Present-Day You (reflective, understanding, capable of change)

### **The Two Wolves**
- The Angry Wolf (reactive rage, suspicion, hurt pride)
- The Wise Wolf (patience, clarity, wisdom)

You become what you consistently feed.

### **The Six Mirrors**
Different aspects of yourself you must face:
1. Physical form
2. Emotions
3. Fears
4. Pride
5. Potential
6. True self

### **The Seven Doors**
Seven ways to defend yourself, only one leads to freedom:
1. Anger
2. Silence
3. Withdrawal
4. Humor
5. Aggression
6. Pride
7. Truth (the only door that leads out)

### **The Golden Rule vs. The Crooked Rule**
- **Golden Rule:** Treat others as you wish you had been treated
- **Crooked Rule:** Treat others as you were treated (perpetuates cycles)

## **EMERGENCY QUESTIONS TO ASK YOURSELF**

When triggered or overwhelmed, ask:

1. **"What am I really feeling?"** (Not just anger—what's underneath?)
2. **"Is this present reality or past trauma?"** (Am I reacting to now or then?)
3. **"What do I actually need right now?"** (Not what I want to demand, but what I need)
4. **"Which voice is speaking?"** (The wounded child or the healing adult?)
5. **"What would the healthy response be?"** (Not what I want to do, but what would be healthy)
6. **"Am I protecting myself or isolating myself?"** (Is this actually helping?)
7. **"What would I tell a friend in this situation?"** (Apply that compassion to yourself)
